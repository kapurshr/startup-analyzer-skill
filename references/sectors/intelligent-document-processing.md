# Sector Thesis: Intelligent Document Processing / Enterprise Document AI

## Market Size
- Global IDP Market (2025): ~$3.2B (pure IDP) to ~$10.6B (broader Document AI)
- Projected (2030): ~$27.6B (Document AI) — MarketsandMarkets
- Projected (2034): ~$43.9B (pure IDP) to $91B (broad) — Precedence Research
- CAGR: 25-34%
- North America: ~47.6% of global market
- BFSI vertical: ~30% of all IDP spending

## Market Structure
Fragmented (90+ vendors), consolidating toward oligopoly in verticals. Three tiers:
1. **Hyperscalers** (Google Doc AI, AWS Textract, Azure Doc Intelligence) — horizontal, API-first, price competition
2. **Platform players** (UiPath $1.67B ARR, Automation Anywhere ~$500M+) — IDP as part of broader RPA
3. **Pure-play IDP** (Instabase ~$50M, ABBYY ~$250M+, Hyperscience ~$30-50M, Rossum ~$45M)

NOT winner-take-all: document types vary by vertical, deployment requirements differ, moderate switching costs.

## Timing
Late land-grab / early consolidation. GenAI created a re-land-grab: vendors integrating LLMs into production-grade enterprise platforms winning. Multiple acquisitions 2024-2025 (Eigen→Sirion, Aisera→Automation Anywhere). Down rounds appearing — flight to quality.

## Tailwinds
- LLM/GenAI revolution improving extraction accuracy
- Agentic AI expanding from "extract data" to "understand and act on documents"
- Digital transformation mandates post-COVID
- Regulatory compliance pressure (KYC/AML, HIPAA, GDPR)

## Headwinds
- LLM commoditization threatening low-end IDP
- Hyperscaler encroachment with bundled "good enough" solutions
- Enterprise budget scrutiny on AI spend
- Hallucination concerns in regulated industries
