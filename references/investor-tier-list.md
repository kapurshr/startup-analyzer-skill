## Tier 3 — Emerging / Unknown (score penalty: -0.25 to Financials dimension)

Less-known firms, regional funds, or angels. Not inherently bad, but less signal value for diligence quality and network effects.

Indicators of Tier 3:
- Fund size under $100M
- Few or no notable exits
- No recognizable portfolio companies
- Primarily angel or family office money
- New fund (Fund I or II) without established partners

## How to Use

When scoring Agent 4's "Investor Quality" sub-dimension:
1. Identify the **lead investor** of the most recent round
2. Check participation from other tiers
3. Apply the scoring adjustment
4. A company with Tier 1 lead + Tier 1/2 participation = strong funding signal
5. A company with only Tier 3 investors at Series B+ is a mild yellow flag (couldn't attract brand-name capital at scale)

Board seats matter too — a Tier 1 partner on the board is more meaningful than a small check from a Tier 1 fund.
